Interviews with the Locals

Me, Gloria, and Alicia interviewed the receptionists at the front desk of our Taipei hotel during our last days in Taiwan. They said that one common tradition across various destinations in Taiwan is the strong discipline in throwing garbage in its proper place and avoiding irresponsible parking. The condition of most buildings in Taiwan are now more modern, but many old, tall buildings still scatter around. Taiwanese society shows its appreciation for the past by respecting traditional culture and preserving the environment like in Jiufen Street, where historical buildings and staircases remain intact, and in Shifen Street, known for its cleanliness and order. The main city destinations in Taiwan include Taipei, Taoyuan, Hualien, and Taichung. New Taipei City is the most modern city among them all, especially with the tourist attraction of Taipei 101. But, other cities are steadily developing too. Intergenerational interactions can be seen through strong respect for the elderly, with older people often being prioritized over the younger ones in public and family settings. Taiwanese people enjoy freedom of belief and expression. Many people follow traditional Chinese religions, specifically Confucianism and Buddhism, which play an important role in their daily lives and cultural practices. There are still various religions in Taiwan that are still believed in.
